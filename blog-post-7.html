5 Tips for Building Websites That Rank on Google

Building a beautiful website isn't enough—you need people to find it. If your website isn't ranking on Google, it's like having a store in the middle of nowhere with no sign pointing to it.

Here are 5 simple SEO strategies that help websites appear higher in Google search results.

What You'll Learn

  • How to optimize your website speed
  • How to write effective meta tags
  • How to choose and use keywords
  • How to optimize images for SEO
  • How to make your site mobile-friendly
💡 The Truth: SEO isn't a magic trick—it's a set of best practices that help search engines understand and rank your content.

Tip 1: Optimize Your Website Speed

1

Page Speed

Google uses page speed as a ranking factor. A slow website hurts your user experience and your rankings.

How to check your speed:

  • Use Google PageSpeed Insights (free tool)
  • Aim for a score of 90+ on mobile
  • Check your Core Web Vitals

How to improve:

  • Compress images: Use tools like TinyPNG or ImageOptim
  • Enable caching: Use browser caching and CDN
  • Minimize code: Remove unnecessary CSS and JavaScript
  • Use a fast hosting provider: Your hosting matters

Pro tip: A 1-second delay in page load time can reduce conversions by 7%.

💡 Quick Win: Compress your images today. It's the fastest way to improve page speed.

Tip 2: Write Effective Meta Tags

2

Meta Tags

Meta tags tell Google what your page is about. They appear in search results and help people decide whether to click.

Two most important meta tags:

  • Title Tag: The headline that appears in search results. Keep it under 60 characters.
  • Meta Description: The short summary under the title. Keep it under 160 characters.

Best practices:

  • Include your primary keyword in both
  • Make it compelling and clickable
  • Be specific about what the page offers

Example of good meta tags:

Title: 5 Tips for Building Websites That Rank on Google | ScaleUp Wealth Hub
Description: Learn simple SEO strategies to help your website rank higher on Google. Covers speed, meta tags, keywords, images, and mobile optimization.
💡 Pro Tip: Your title tag is the first thing people see. Make it irresistible.

Tip 3: Use Keywords Strategically

3

Keywords

Keywords are the words and phrases people type into Google. Using them correctly helps Google match your content to search queries.

Where to use keywords:

  • ✅ Title tag (most important)
  • ✅ H1 heading (main page title)
  • ✅ H2 and H3 subheadings
  • ✅ First paragraph of content
  • ✅ Image alt text
  • ✅ URL (if possible)

Tools for finding keywords:

  • Google Keyword Planner: Free tool from Google
  • Ubersuggest: Free keyword ideas
  • AnswerThePublic: See what people are searching

Pro tip: Don't "keyword stuff"—use keywords naturally. Google is smart enough to detect overuse.

Tip 4: Optimize Your Images

4

Image Optimization

Images can slow down your site and hurt SEO if not optimized properly.

Three things to do:

  1. Compress images: Reduce file size without losing quality
  2. Use descriptive filenames: Instead of "IMG_1234.jpg," use "seo-tips-for-websites.jpg"
  3. Add alt text: Describe the image for accessibility and SEO

Tools for image optimization:

  • TinyPNG: Compress PNG and JPG images
  • Squoosh: Google's image compression tool
  • ImageOptim: Mac app for image compression

Pro tip: Use WebP format when possible—it's smaller than JPG/PNG.

💡 Quick Win: Use descriptive alt text for all your images. It helps both SEO and accessibility.

Tip 5: Make Your Site Mobile-Friendly

5

Mobile Optimization

Google uses mobile-first indexing, meaning it primarily looks at the mobile version of your site. If your site isn't mobile-friendly, you won't rank well.

How to check if your site is mobile-friendly:

  • Use Google's Mobile-Friendly Test
  • Check your site on your phone
  • Look for issues like tiny text, buttons too close, and horizontal scrolling

Best practices for mobile:

  • Responsive design: Your site should adapt to any screen size
  • Readable font size: At least 16px for body text
  • Tap-friendly buttons: At least 44px for touch targets
  • Easy navigation: Simple menu that works on touch

Pro tip: Test your site on multiple devices and browsers before launching.

✅ Pro Tip: A mobile-friendly site is no longer optional—it's required for SEO success.

Quick SEO Checklist

  • ✅ Page speed: Score 90+ on Google PageSpeed Insights
  • ✅ Title tags: Under 60 characters with keyword
  • ✅ Meta descriptions: Under 160 characters with keyword
  • ✅ Keywords: Used naturally in headings and content
  • ✅ Images: Compressed with descriptive alt text
  • ✅ Mobile-friendly: Pass Google's Mobile-Friendly Test

Bonus: Additional SEO Tips

  • Create quality content: Google rewards helpful, well-written content
  • Build backlinks: Links from other sites boost your authority
  • Use internal links: Link to other pages on your site
  • Add a sitemap: Helps Google find all your pages
  • Use HTTPS: Google prefers secure websites

Final Thoughts

SEO isn't complicated—but it does require consistent effort. Start with these 5 tips, and you'll see improvements in your search rankings over time.

Remember, SEO is a marathon, not a sprint. The best time to start optimizing was yesterday. The second best time is today.

🚀 Ready to Rank? Start with one tip today. Pick the easiest one and implement it. Then move to the next.
